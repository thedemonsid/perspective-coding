## 💡 Key Concepts

### Data Packets: Your Digital Parcels

Just like how a long letter might be sent in multiple envelopes, large files (like videos or images) are split into smaller packets. Each packet:

- Contains a piece of the data
- Knows where it's going (destination IP)
- Knows where it came from (source IP)
- Has a sequence number (so it can be reassembled)

### IP Addresses: The Internet's Home Addresses

Every device on the internet has a unique address called an IP address. It's like your home address, but for your devices! For example:

- IPv4 addresses look like: 192.168.1.1
- IPv6 addresses look like: 2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334

### DNS: The Internet's Phone Directory

Instead of remembering IP addresses like 142.250.190.78, DNS lets us use friendly names like "google.com". It's like having a phone book that converts names to numbers automatically!
